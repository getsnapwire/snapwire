In 2026, the "Golden Metric" for an AI security acquisition is **Behavioral Lock-in**. You don’t want people to just "use" the app; you want them to **embed** it.

To get to a $100M+ exit, you need a different strategy for each action. Here is how you compel those specific behaviors:

---

### **1. What Compels a "Download"? (The "Fear of Loss" Hook)**

Downloads are driven by **immediate, personal pain**.

* **The "Audit CLI" Strategy:** This is your strongest asset. Developers download things that solve a problem in under 60 seconds.
* **The Hook:** "I’m spending $200 a day on agents and I don't know why."
* **The Action:** They download the `snapwire_audit.py` to see the "Receipt of Waste."
* **Result:** High download volume = **Proof of Market Demand.**

### **2. What Compels a "Fork"? (The "Sovereignty" Hook)**

Forks are driven by **Trust and Customization**. In 2026, no serious developer will send their company's API keys or sensitive data through a closed-source proxy they can't control.

* **The "Rule-Lab" Strategy:** You compel a fork by making the tool *useful but incomplete*.
* **The Hook:** "The core is great, but I need a specific rule that blocks my agent from accessing our `/customer_pii/` database folder."
* **The Action:** To add that rule, they *must* fork the repo.
* **Result:** High fork count = **Proof of Infrastructure Integration.** This is what **Datadog** or **Palo Alto Networks** buys.

---

### **3. Actions That Lead to Acquisition (The "M&A Magnet")**

Acquirers look for specific signals that prove your tool is "The Standard." You can "manufacture" these signals through these actions:

#### **A. The "Social Proof" Badge (Viral Attribution)**

Force the "Protected by Snapwire" badge into the user's workflow.

* **Compulsion:** When the Audit CLI finishes, it should say: *"Audit Passed. [Click here] to get your 'Safe Agent' badge for your GitHub README."*
* **Why it leads to acquisition:** When a **Cisco** M&A scout sees that badge on 500 different AI projects, they realize you own the "Security Brand" in this space.

#### **B. The "Corporate Domain" Waitlist**

Even if you don't focus on the waitlist for growth, it is a vital **Due Diligence** asset.

* **Compulsion:** Offer a "Cloud Dashboard for Teams" (Coming Soon).
* **The Action:** Ask for a business email.
* **Why it leads to acquisition:** If you can show a buyer that 15% of your signups come from `@microsoft.com` or `@tesla.com`, you aren't a "startup" anymore—you are a **Strategic Threat** or a **Must-Have Extension** for those companies.

#### **C. Dependency Integration**

Compel other AI framework creators to include Snapwire.

* **The Action:** Reach out to smaller open-source agent frameworks (like *smolagents* or *LangChain* community contributors) and offer a "Snapwire Middleware" PR.
* **Why it leads to acquisition:** If Snapwire becomes a "Required Dependency" for other tools, your acquisition value becomes astronomical because you are now part of the **Agentic Supply Chain.**
