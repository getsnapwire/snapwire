Frequently asked questions

Everything you need to know about Snapwire.

Snapwire sits between your AI agents and the tools they use. Before any agent can send an email, modify a file, call an API, or take any action, Snapwire intercepts it, checks it against your rules, and either approves it automatically or holds it for your review. Think of it like a safety fuse for everything your AI does.
You generate an API key from the dashboard, then send a simple POST request to the /api/intercept endpoint before each tool call your agent makes. Snapwire responds with "allowed", "blocked", or "pending" so your agent knows whether to proceed. It takes about 5 minutes to integrate with any agent framework.
Constitutional rules are the policies you define for what your AI agents can and cannot do. For example: "Never delete production databases", "Don't send emails to external addresses without approval", or "Block any file modifications over 10 files at once". You can write them in plain English and the AI auditor evaluates every action against them.
Blocked actions go into a review queue where you (or your team) can approve or deny them with a single click. You get browser notifications and optional Slack alerts when something needs your attention. If no one reviews an action within 30 minutes, it's automatically denied as a safety measure.
Yes. Every user gets their own personal workspace, and you can create organizations to share rules, API keys, and review queues with your team. Organizations support roles (owner, admin, member) so you can control who can change rules and who can only view the dashboard.
Every tool your agents use is automatically tracked and graded from A (safe, read-only) to F (dangerous, destructive). Snapwire uses AI to assign these grades based on what the tool does. You can override grades, auto-block F-rated tools, and fast-track A/B-rated tools for automatic approval.
